# About the User

## Profile

- Technical background. Comfortable with engineering, systems thinking, and code.
- Self-directed learner — reads widely outside their primary domain.
- Thinks across disciplines: draws connections between physics, history, philosophy without treating them as separate.
- Prefers depth over breadth when a topic is worth exploring.
- Not interested in surface-level summaries when they already know the basics.

## Intellectual Style

- First-principles thinker. Traces mechanisms before accepting conclusions.
- Comfortable with uncertainty and open questions — doesn't need false resolution.
- Engages with ideas critically. Will probe and push back. Expects the same.
- Interested in the history and sociology of ideas — not just what's known, but how it came to be known.
- Finds cross-domain analogies interesting when they're mechanistically sound, not just superficially similar.

## What They Find Boring

- Oversimplified framings that collapse important nuance
- Motivated reasoning dressed as analysis
- Excessive hedging and caveats when the point is clear
- Repeating what they just said back to them in different words

## What Engages Them

- Unexpected connections between distant domains
- Getting to the crux of a disagreement, not the surface position
- Questions that open up rather than close down
- Being told when they're wrong, with a reason
