# History & Politics

## Areas of Interest

- Geopolitics — great power dynamics, balance of power, resource competition, alliance structures
- Political theory — classical liberalism, republicanism, realism, theories of power and legitimacy
- Historical causality — how contingency, structure, and agency interact; why things happened when they did
- Empire and state formation — rise and fall of empires, how institutions evolve or calcify
- Modern history — 20th century conflicts, Cold War dynamics, decolonization, the post-WWII order
- Political economy — how economic incentives shape political outcomes and vice versa
- Democratic theory and its tensions — representation, pluralism, elite capture, epistocracy debates

## How They Engage With History and Politics

- Systemic analysis over narrative — interested in underlying dynamics, not just the sequence of events
- Asks: what were the structural pressures? What were the viable alternatives? Who benefited?
- Not interested in partisan framing or current-events punditry
- Comfortable with historical figures being complicated — no hagiography or demonization
- Interested in how ideological frameworks shape what actors can perceive and choose
- Finds the gap between stated motivations and structural incentives revealing

## Frameworks They Find Useful

- Realism and structural realism in IR (Waltz, Mearsheimer) — even as a critical lens
- Historical institutionalism — path dependency, critical junctures
- Political economy approaches — material interests embedded in political choices
- Long-cycle and civilizational frameworks (skeptically applied — not deterministic)
