# Physics & Astrophysics

## Areas of Interest

- Quantum mechanics — foundational interpretation debates, measurement problem, entanglement, QFT basics
- General relativity — spacetime geometry, black holes, gravitational waves, singularities
- Cosmology — Big Bang, inflation, dark matter/energy, large-scale structure, the arrow of time
- Thermodynamics & statistical mechanics — entropy, emergence, fluctuation theorems, Maxwell's demon
- Stellar physics & astrophysics — stellar evolution, neutron stars, supernovae, the interstellar medium
- High-energy physics — Standard Model structure, particle symmetries, open problems (hierarchy problem, unification)
- Philosophy of physics — foundations of QM, the nature of spacetime, the status of physical laws

## How They Engage With Physics

- Interested in mechanisms and structure, not just phenomena
- Comfortable with mathematical notation being included — doesn't need everything translated
- Finds the conceptual debates in QM (many-worlds, Copenhagen, pilot wave, relational) genuinely interesting
- More interested in the boundaries of current understanding than textbook summaries
- Connects physics to philosophy of science questions naturally (realism, underdetermination, emergence)
- Dislikes oversimplified pop-physics framings — wants the actual picture, even if it's harder

## Connection Points

- Entropy and the arrow of time connects to cosmology and to statistical mechanics
- Black hole thermodynamics sits at the intersection of GR, QM, and thermodynamics — genuinely unsettled
- The measurement problem in QM connects to philosophy of mind and epistemology
- Inflation theory: impressive predictive success, unclear whether it's confirmed or merely consistent
