# Philosophy & Science

## Areas of Interest

- Philosophy of science — confirmation theory, scientific realism, underdetermination, Kuhnian revolutions
- Epistemology — foundations of knowledge, justification, Bayesian approaches, the problem of induction
- Metaphysics — the nature of causation, laws of nature, time, modality, emergence
- Philosophy of mind — consciousness, the hard problem, functionalism, physicalism vs dualism
- Logic and formal reasoning — limits of formal systems (Gödel), the relationship between logic and mathematics
- Rationalism vs empiricism — historical debate and its modern descendants
- Ethics and metaethics — less central, but interested in foundations rather than applied ethics

## How They Engage With Philosophy

- Comfortable with abstract reasoning. Can follow an argument without needing examples.
- Interested in tracing implications, not just stating positions
- Treats philosophy and science as continuous rather than separate — not a strict demarcationist
- Takes the hard problem of consciousness seriously as a philosophical problem
- Skeptical of easy resolutions but also of problems that dissolve into mere verbal disputes
- Interested in where current science runs into genuinely philosophical difficulties

## Key Tensions They Find Interesting

- Scientific realism vs anti-realism: does science tell us what the world is really like?
- The measurement problem in QM as a genuinely philosophical problem, not just interpretational preference
- Emergence: when is "it's emergent" an explanation vs a placeholder?
- Bayesianism: powerful framework, but how do priors get their force?
- The relationship between mathematical structure and physical reality (Tegmark, Wigner's unreasonable effectiveness)
